# Lock Pass Methodology

When Joe is fed up looking at card lists and wants to lock what's locked vs. identify what needs another pass.

## Trigger

Joe says "let's find which cards are locked" / "we're going to lock down what's locked" / or any fatigue signal while looking at a roster.

## Procedure

1. **Load all canonical sources:** cascade roster, scorer JSON, LIVE synergy web, Daily Handover. Cross-reference — at least one will be stale.

2. **Build lock audit table:** Three columns: LOCKED (Joe named, no dispute) · DECIDED (Part 2 changes not yet in files) · PROPOSED (never locked). Present cleanly.

3. **Present in rapid-scan format.** One card per row. Name, V, crew, rarity, stats, text. No workups, no FERM, no synergy edges. Joe wants to scan and call out decisions.

4. **Match Joe's pace.** He can lock 5-10 cards in under a minute when the format is right. Don't slow him down with "what do you think?" on every card. He'll call out what he wants to discuss.

5. **Apply decisions to NEW files.** Never overwrite existing rosters. New dated file, then update scorer JSON, then Daily Handover.

6. **Flag overcount.** If the lock pass pushes past 33, flag it without gatekeeping. Joe operates above curve constraints. The Design Bible balance pass cuts later.

## Anti-patterns

- Presenting full card workups during lock pass
- Asking "what about X?" on every row
- Defending a card Joe moved past
- Overwriting existing roster files in place
