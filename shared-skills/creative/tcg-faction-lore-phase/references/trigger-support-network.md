# Trigger Support Network — Worked Example

Full support personnel brainstorm for a professional-operations faction. Each role includes backstory and a card-design hook. Created 2026-06-03 from Joe+Paul Trigger session.

## Medical

### Dr. Marcus Webb — Disgraced Surgeon
Lost his medical license in 1954 — drunk, crossed the center line on Woodward, killed a teenager. Did eighteen months. Came out unemployable, radioactive. The Commission keeps him on retainer, kept boozed up (Canadian Club, a fifth a day) and sobered up with black coffee and amphetamines when a Hitter comes in bleeding. Steady hands return under pressure — muscle memory from a decade of trauma surgery at Receiving Hospital. Operates out of a back room at the Cadillac's Continental floor. No records. Better drunk than most surgeons are sober, and he knows it.

**Card hook:** Risky heal — coin flip or die roll. Works or fizzles. *"Steady hands. Most of the time."*

## Transportation

### Bernie Davis — Davis Auto Sales
Used car lot on Grand River. "Pre-Owned, Pre-Inspected, Pre-War." Cash-heavy business, perfect for laundering. Clean cars on the lot, dirty cars out the back. Same vehicle from a Contract Tuesday is on the lot Thursday with new paint and a fresh VIN. Guy at the DMV files paperwork that looks correct. Garage handles plate swaps, steam-cleaning (evidence removal), a crusher for the unsalvageable. Crates of Villium arrive in trunks of "just traded in" cars. Adjacent to Stiffs territory — some best customers are cops who need clean cars.

**Card hook:** Vehicle tutor — search deck for weapon/equipment. Or spell giving Hustle ("fresh wheels").

## Documents

### Margot Chen — Forger
Chinese-American, second generation. Father ran a print shop in San Francisco's Chinatown — forgery learned as mercy (immigration papers for Angel Island). Post-war, moved to Detroit. Produces: passports, driver's licenses, press credentials, police badges, firefighter IDs, city council letterhead, utility worker ID. Documents are good because she has access to the real things — the Triggers know people in every agency. Works above a print shop on Cass Avenue. Builds full identities — ID, uniform, work order, vehicle with magnetic signage.

**Card hook (Joe's idea):** Splash cost reduction — "target Trigger minion costs 1V less this turn." Cross-faction enabler. Mechanically fresh.

## Target Preparation (Etta's Team)

### Etta — Target Research Lead
Hybrid private eye. Builds the file: schedules, habits, security, vulnerabilities. Shadows marks personally for high-priority Contracts. A Professional who doesn't prep gets her file. A bad file is a dead Professional. She carries that weight.

**Card hook:** Scry/tutor — "look at opponent's hand" or "search deck for Mark spell."

### Handyman (Unnamed — Role-Based)
Plumber, electrician, maintenance man. Walks sites days before a hit: which doors stick, which windows open, fuse box location, exit route verification. Plants weapons in advance. Locks exits open. Every building has one, nobody looks twice. Former tradesman — union card, calluses, the tool belt isn't a prop.

**Card hook:** Ambush enabler — "target minion loses Taunt this turn" or board prep spell.

### Wireman (Unnamed — Role-Based)
Phone company lineman — Detroit Bell. Knows the infrastructure: junction boxes, how to tap without breaking signal, where to plant bugs. Does counter-surveillance — sweeps Penobscot and Cadillac monthly. Army Signal Corps in the Ardennes, same work, better pay now. Detroit Bell truck, clipboard with real work orders.

**Card hook:** Intel spell — "look at opponent's hand, choose one to discard." Or counter-surveillance Ambush.

## Equipment

### Carlo Abruzzo — Quartermaster (Guns & Kit)
30 years at the bench. Russian-trained. Runs the Range. Every weapon tagged by contractor, specialty, job type. "Close work" = snub-nose. "Sending a message" = Chicago Typewriter. "Accident" = something else entirely.

**Card hook:** Weapon tutor or equipment buff.

## Wardrobe

### Alberto Rossi — Tailor (High-End)
Rossi & Son, Woodward Avenue since 1928. Son died at Chosin Reservoir. Bulletproof suits that drape like silk — stops .38 at ten feet. Three patents through Delaware shell company. Gerald's grey suit, twenty years.

**Card hook:** Equipment/Armor grant — "equip target minion with Rossi Suit (+0/+2)."

### Celeste Moreau — Wardrobe Mistress (Practical)
Former Broadway wardrobe (*Oklahoma!*, *Carousel*). Left New York in '55 after a scandal. Builds costumes to spec: waiter's apron concealing a holster, coveralls with reinforced pockets, hard hat that passes inspection. Researches — knows what a Detroit Edison lineman actually wears. Shop above a closed vaudeville house on Cass. Racks organized by profession, not show. Gets measurements from Etta, delivers to Cadillac. Clothes come back bloody, she burns them.

**Card hook (Joe's idea):** Cost manipulation — splash reduction. "Celeste dressed him to fit right in." Or Disguise token: "cannot be targeted until they attack."

## Disposal

### Mr. Fox — The Cleaner
Play on Mr. Wolf from Pulp Fiction. Solo operator. Jobs that can't have witnesses. When Il Trio aren't enough. Nobody knows what he actually does. People who've seen him work aren't available for comment.

**Card hook:** Legendary removal — exile target minion, no death trigger.

### Il Trio — Standard Cleanup
Three as one (two brothers and a cousin, supposedly). Panel van: "Metropolitan Janitorial Services." 30-minute response. Lye, canvas tarp, bone saw, industrial cleaner, paint, plaster, cover-story props. After: same diner, same pie, same booth. Talk about baseball.

**Card hook:** Board wipe — "destroy all Marked minions."

## Training

### Walt Kowalski — Range Instructor
Sergeant First Class (Ret.), 101st Airborne, 506th PIR. Normandy, Holland, Bastogne. Bronze Star, permanent limp. Tried civilian life — didn't take. Gerald recruited him in '52: train killers in tactics, room clearing, cover and movement, reading a room. Off-site facility in industrial corridor. "Slow is smooth, smooth is fast." Court doesn't train with him — he thinks that's why someday one of them will die in a room they didn't know how to exit.

**Card hook:** Training spell — permanent +1 ATK or battlecry buff.

## Hospitality

### Continental Floor Staff (Cadillac Hotel, 14th Floor)
- **James** — Concierge. 15 years at the Book-Cadillac before recruitment. Knows every Professional by face, preference, alias. Gatekeeper.
- **Mick** — Bartender. Irish, post-war emigrant. Pours, listens, repeats nothing.
- **René** — Sommelier. Claims Ritz Paris before the occupation. The private booths have curtains. The private booths have ears.
- **Mrs. Cullen** — Housekeeper. Widow of a Trigger killed in '51. Asked for a job instead of the pension. Changes sheets, empties ashtrays, never opens a drawer.
- **Hector** — Doorman. Built like a Bruiser, dressed like a diplomat. Only staff authorized to put hands on a guest.

**Card hooks:** Support spells — draw/discard (concierge), minor heal (housekeeper), protection (sommelier's booth).

## Legal / Alibi

### Dewey, Cheatem & Howe
On retainer. A Professional gets pinched, a lawyer arrives. "My client was at dinner with associates." The associates are Management operators. The receipt is backdated. The firm actually exists, actually files — "estate planning consultation" = sanctioned hit, "real estate acquisition" = territory expansion.

**Card hook:** Not a card — setting texture. Could be referenced in spell flavor text.
