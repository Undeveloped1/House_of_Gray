# Paul's Amazon FBA SOP Conventions

These are Joe/Paul-specific conventions that layer on top of the general process-documentation workflow. Apply them when documenting Paul's business processes (Amazon FBA shipping, operations, training guides).

## Voice Note-Specific Patterns

Joe sends voice messages frequently. The system transcribes them automatically. Key patterns:
- "Commintory" = "Inventory" (voice-to-text distortion)
- "skews" = "SKUs"
- Names and codes (IAH3, ABE8) — preserve exactly as spoken
- Inside jokes (Mr. Mackie "mmkay") — Joe likes these preserved in the doc voice. Keep them.

## Critical Warnings

Use ⚠️ callouts for steps where getting it wrong costs money, time, or inventory:

```markdown
- **⚠️ CRITICAL: [What happens if you get this wrong.]** [How to do it right.]
```

Placement rules:
- First occurrence: bold warning in the step itself
- Reinforcement: repeat at the point where the consequence surfaces (e.g., warn about equal box distribution at both the packing step AND the shipment approval step where the $300 fee appears)

## Paul-Specific Pitfalls

- Don't guess UI element names. If Joe's voice transcription is garbled, ask or flag as open item rather than inventing a plausible button name.
- Don't merge phases too aggressively. Joe thinks in phases (prep → template → Seller Central → packing). Keep his mental model.
- Keep the voice. If Joe says "bad time, mmkay" — that goes in the SOP. It's his document, his voice.
- "Uneven boxes = $300+ placement fee" hits harder than "Ensure even distribution."
