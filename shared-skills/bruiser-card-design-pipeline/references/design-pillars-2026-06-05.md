# Five Crests — Design Pillars (Locked 2026-06-05)

## Faction Mixing Penalty

- Splashing an off-faction card costs **+1V** baseline
- Splashing a **rival faction** costs **+2V** (e.g. Trigger splashing Duster)
- Penalty is **applied automatically** based on the hero you select
- Hero card determines which faction is "in-lane" → all other factions incur the penalty

## Faction Loyalty — "Pull" Not "Punish"

- Synergy within faction should be so beneficial you're *pulled* toward staying in-lane
- NOT punitive — you CAN splash, it just costs more and you lose internal synergies
- Cross-faction synergy scarcity: cards say "target friendly Bruiser minion" not "target friendly minion"
- Tribal design: each faction cares about its own (Slivers analog)
- Some universal "Rodman" cards will exist as cross-faction staples

## Hero Restrictions

- Hero abilities target only their own faction's minions
- No Bruiser hero pumping a Trigger minion

## Hearthstone Is the Structural Analog

- Shared Villium resource (no colored mana), no mana screw, always curve to 5V by turn 5
- Faction identity enforced through mechanical access + blind spots + cost penalties
- MTG color pie is a reference, not a template

## Success Criteria for Five Crests Decks

1. **Curve coherence** — cards at each V-band progress toward a win condition
2. **Mechanical density** — enough cards doing the faction's thing that identity fires reliably
3. **Blind spot pressure** — weaknesses are real enough you WANT to splash
4. **Win condition clarity** — engine outputs lethal, not value for value's sake
5. **Interaction points** — opponent has meaningful decisions (Ambush windows, Cover positioning, hidden info)

## Design Philosophy

- Power creep isn't the concern — cohesiveness and fun are
- "The game should feel like it sounds" — Joe
- Perfect balance is not the goal — cohesive identity and enjoyable play patterns are
