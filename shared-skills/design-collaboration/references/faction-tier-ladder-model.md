# Faction Tier-Ladder Organizational Model

**Established:** Trigger faction build, 2026-06-03
**Applies to:** Organized crime factions with clear social hierarchy (Trigger, possibly future factions)

## When to Use This Model

Use a **tier ladder** instead of **divisions-by-function** when a faction's identity is fundamentally about *vertical mobility* — who you are depends on where you are on the ladder. This is distinct from Bruiser's crew structure, where crews represent different operational domains (Dock/Yard, Hall, Street, Spread) that coexist horizontally.

Trigger is a tier ladder. Every member's identity is defined by what rung they occupy. Movement between rungs is earned through violence, reputation, and money.

## Tier Ladder Structure (Trigger example)

| Tier | Name | Who | Visibility | Card Role |
|------|------|-----|------------|-----------|
| 1 | **The Help** | Civilian-adjacent. Gunsmiths, armorers, drivers, cleanup. Well-paid, not made. | Invisible — they're the help, nobody looks at them | Low-V utility, weapon-gen, Arsenal keywords, Ambushes |
| 2 | **Street** | Disposable hitters. Got a pistol, wants to make money. Robbery-gone-wrong guys. | Anonymous — they're nobody until the job goes wrong | Low-V aggressive minions, Hustle, cheap and expendable |
| 3 | **The Hitters** | Professional standard. Steady hands. Lucy's tier. | Professional — known in the trade, not to civilians | Mid-V Contract engine, the backbone |
| 4 | **The Names** | Ascended. Flashy. Gold-etched. Known — and targeted for it. | **The point.** Everyone knows who they are | High-V/Rare+, Barrage tier |
| 5 | **The Ghosts** | Above even the Names. Invisible. Report only to Gerald. | Zero. They don't exist | Legendary only. Top-end closers |
| 6 | **The Switchboard** | Upper management. Gerald's apparatus. Morning Herald, travel agency, law firm fronts. | Corporate front — hiding in plain sight | Spell-heavy. Draw, scry, tutor, intel. Few/no minion bodies |

## Key Properties of Tier Ladders

### Vertical Mobility as Identity
Every card tells you where on the ladder its character stands. A Street hitter isn't just "a cheaper Hitter" — they're a different kind of person with different ambitions, different risks, different flavor text. The ladder IS the faction's story.

### Visibility Increases with Rank
Bottom tiers are invisible. Middle tiers are known in the trade. Top tiers are either famous (Names) or don't exist (Ghosts). This maps to mechanical identity: the more visible you are, the more you're a target, and the more your card needs to deliver on that promise.

### Front vs. Back Office
The Switchboard (upper management) doesn't fight. They're spell-heavy infrastructure — draw, scry, tutor, intel. The Help doesn't fight either — they enable. The fighting happens in tiers 2-5. This creates natural card-type distribution: minions cluster in the middle, spells cluster at the management tier, weapons/enablers cluster at the support tier.

### Generational Tension as Cross-Cutting Thread
Old Way (omertà, patience, setup) vs. New Way (splash, go-loud) runs ACROSS all tiers, not within them. Some Street hitters are Old Way (quiet professionals doing clean work). Some Names are Old Way (earned their gold but stay subdued). This prevents the ladder from becoming a simple "low tier = new, high tier = old" mapping.

## When NOT to Use a Tier Ladder

- When a faction's identity is about *operational domains* (Bruiser: docks, gyms, streets, speakeasies — different places, different work)
- When a faction is defined by *philosophy* rather than hierarchy (Faceless: belief structure)
- When a faction has flat or non-hierarchical organization (Skiver: survival network)

## Design Implications

### Card Type Distribution
The ladder creates natural breaks:
- Tiers 1-2 (Help/Street): Enablers and cheap bodies. Weapons, Ambushes, low-cost minions.
- Tiers 3-4 (Hitters/Names): The curve backbone. Mid-to-high-V minions, Contract engine.
- Tier 5 (Ghosts): Legendaries only. Top-end.
- Tier 6 (Switchboard): Spells. Draw/tutor/intel. Almost no minion bodies.

### Rarity Distribution
Rarity follows the ladder naturally:
- Low tiers → Common/Uncommon
- Mid tiers → Uncommon/Rare
- Top tiers → Rare/Legendary

### Keyword Distribution
- Faction keywords (Marked, Contract, Paid, Armed, Honed) span tiers 2-4
- Universal keywords (Hustle, Cloak, Stride) appear across the faction
- Elite keywords (Barrage) are tier 4-5 only
- Tier 6 gets no combat keywords — spells don't fight

### NOT Creating Mini-Factions
The risk of a tier ladder is that each tier feels like its own mini-faction. The guardrail: every tier should share the faction's core mechanical identity (for Trigger: weapons, Contracts, direct damage). A Ghost legendary should feel like a more powerful version of what a Street hitter does, not a completely different game.
