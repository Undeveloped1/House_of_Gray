# Faction Ecosystem Positioning Methodology

## When to use

When designing or redesigning a faction in a multi-faction TCG — BEFORE any card design, function registry, or synergy web work begins.

## The method

### Step 1: Map the current ecosystem

List every faction with its current archetype and speed:

| Faction | Lane | Speed | Partner |
|---------|------|-------|---------|
| Faction A | Aggro-midrange | T4–7 | Faction C |
| Faction B | Swarm aggro | T3–6 | Faction D |
| Faction C | ? | ? | Faction A |
| Faction D | Control-combo | T5–9 | Faction B |

### Step 2: Identify the gap

Look at the speed/lane columns. What's missing? In Five Crests, Bruiser (aggro-midrange) + Skiver (swarm aggro) + Duster (control-combo) + Faceless (control-midrange) left midrange tempo completely unfilled. The gap IS the faction's role.

### Step 3: Define matchups against every faction

For the faction being designed, define how it plays against each opponent:

- **vs Faction A:** How does it handle their game plan? Favored/even/unfavored?
- **vs Faction B:** etc.
- **vs Partner faction (mirror):** Even matchup is the default target.

Each matchup needs a one-sentence reason, not just a label.

### Step 4: Define pairing synergy

The faction's partner is the other half of its two-faction team. Answer:

- What does YOUR faction provide that the partner lacks? (e.g., removal, card draw, weapons)
- What does the PARTNER provide that your faction lacks? (e.g., bodies, face pressure, Taunt)
- Why do they work together better than either works alone?

### Step 5: Lock the role BEFORE designing

The ecosystem role is a constraint that shapes everything downstream:

- Crew distribution target (which crews get card slots)
- Archetype split (how heroes differ within the same role)
- Blind spots (what the faction CANNOT do, because another faction does it)
- Speed band (turns 5–7 vs turns 8–12)

## Anti-pattern

Optimizing for internal synergy web completeness at the expense of ecosystem role. A faction can have a perfectly connected synergy web and still fail because it's playing the wrong game for its slot in the ecosystem. The synergy web serves the role, not the other way around.

## Example: Trigger positioning (2026-06-06)

**Gap identified:** Nobody owns midrange. Bruiser is big-body aggro, Skiver is swarm aggro, Duster is reactive control, Faceless is control-mill. Trigger fills the midrange tempo slot.

**Matchups:**
- vs Bruiser: Even. Weapons and Marked removal punch through Taunt, but Bruiser out-stats you.
- vs Skiver: Favored. Marked removal picks off key pieces, Cloak protects against Swarm.
- vs Duster: Even. Intel plays around Blur, but Blur can blank Contract sequencing.
- vs Faceless: Even to slightly unfavored. Removal handles Blessed bodies, but Purge mills key pieces.

**Pairing with Bruiser:** Trigger provides removal, weapons, sequencing. Bruiser provides bodies and face pressure. Classic "removal + beaters" hybrid.

**Role locked:** Midrange tempo with Contract engine. Turns 5–7. Not the fastest, not the slowest. Efficient sequencing over overwhelming board presence.
