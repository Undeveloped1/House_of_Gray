# Skiver Character Deep-Dive Template

The Skiver faction received a full character pass before hero design. This reference captures the methodology Joe wants replicated for Bruiser and all future factions.

Source: `docs/archive/paul_vault_design_2026-05-29/Skiver_Bruiser_Brainstorm_Clean_v2.md`

## What the Skiver pass produced

1. **Faction thesis** — one-line identity, class position, relationship to other factions
2. **Inner circle archetype mapping** — Robin Hood band: Marisol=Robin, Joseph=Little John, Diana=Friar Tuck, Fatty=Azeem, Corey=Will Scarlet
3. **Individual character bios** per inner circle member:
   - Role in the faction
   - Origin story with specific dates
   - Motivation and defining choice
   - Relationship to other characters
   - Historical/cultural grounding
   - Mechanical lane (keyword ownership)
4. **Founding story** — specific event (1954 bad winter), 7-year build to present day
5. **Internal rift** — founders vs opportunists, narrative tension
6. **Territory map** — six neighborhoods, third dimension (sewers/rooftops)
7. **Second wave** — post-founding joiners, why they joined

## Per-character bio template

```
### [Name] — [Archetype]

**Role:** One sentence.
**Origin:** Where they came from. Specific years. Specific events.
**Motivation:** What drives them. The defining choice.
**Relationship:** Who they're connected to and how.
**Historical grounding:** Real history under the character.
**Mechanical lane:** Which keyword they own.
```

## Process order (locked)

1. Faction thesis → inner circle archetype mapping
2. Founding story with timeline
3. Individual bios (one per session section for Joe review)
4. Internal dynamics and tensions
5. Territory and geography
6. Keyword lane assignment
7. Capo ability design (passive/active/ultimate)

Do NOT design Capo abilities before Steps 1-6 are complete. Character determines mechanics.
