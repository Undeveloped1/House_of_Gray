## The Sufficiency of the Present Moment

**Scripture:** "Therefore do not be anxious about tomorrow, for tomorrow will be anxious for itself. Sufficient for the day is its own trouble." — Matthew 6:34 (ESV)

**Stoic:** "Don't let the future disturb you. You will meet it, if you must, with the same weapons of reason which today arm you against the present." — Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 7.8

*Both refuse to let the imagined weight of tomorrow steal the strength needed for today. Neither denies the future — they deny its right to govern the present.*

**Reflect:**
- When has anticipating a difficulty caused you more suffering than the difficulty itself?
- Prudent planning and anxious rehearsal can look identical from the outside. How do you tell which one you're doing?
- Does "sufficient for the day" ever become a cover for avoiding hard decisions that genuinely require long-range thinking?
