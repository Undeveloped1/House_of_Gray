# Faction Play-Style Articulation Formula

When Joe asks "what play style does this faction align with" or "what archetype is this," use this formula. Do not lead with MTG deck packages — derive from the faction's own tier structure first.

## The Formula

**`[tempo category] + [distinguishing mechanic]`**

Not "midrange" — "methodical midrange with inevitability."
Not "control" — "precision removal with Contract payoff."
Not "aggro" — "relentless aggro with swarm recursion."

The distinguishing mechanic is what makes it a Five Crests faction, not a color pair.

## The Derivation Order

1. **What does each tier do?** — Each tier has a mechanical job.
2. **What is the cross-tier flow?** — How do the tiers connect?
3. **What tempo does this produce?** — Short games, long games, or midrange?
4. **What is the distinguishing mechanic?** — The ONE thing this faction does that no other faction does.
5. **Name it.** — `[tempo] + [distinguisher]`. Then write the one-sentence definition.
6. **MAP to known TCG vocabulary LAST.** — Only after the faction's own identity is clear.

## Trigger Example (2026-06-04)

**Tier jobs:** Management = intel/contracts. Hitters = execution/removal. Street = bodies/screen. Help = buffs/support.

**Cross-tier flow:** Management generates Contract → Help arms Hitter → Hitter completes → Street covers exit.

**Tempo:** Deploys early (Street/Help), sets up mid (Management), executes late (Hitters). Wins turns 6-9.

**Distinguisher:** Inevitability through Contract completion. Every turn advances. Opponent sees clock but cannot stop all of it.

**Formulation:** "Methodical midrange with inevitability."

**MTG map:** Orzhov with Dimir inevitability. Hearthstone: Value Rogue.

## Anti-Pattern

Do NOT open with "Here are four MTG package options." Start from the faction's tiers. Joe corrects this immediately.
