# Challenger Audit — Tabitha Autobiography Project

**Date:** 2026-07-02
**Auditor:** Challenger agent (Hermes, deepseek-v4-pro)
**Status:** Phase 1, Day 11/12 (split-brain state)

## Findings

### 1. Two State Files, Out of Sync

- **Live (profile path):** `~/.hermes/profiles/tabitha/docs/Tabitha/projects/autobiography-state.json` — day 12, contains full interview questions from the checklist
- **Stale (`/root/docs/`):** `/root/docs/Tabitha/projects/autobiography-state.json` — day 11, contains topic LABELS not questions ("Role models growing up", "First experience with death/loss", "What childhood smelled like")
- The cron script only writes to the profile-path copy. The `/root/docs/` copy is a zombie.
- Any agent reading the wrong path gets wrong data.

### 2. Two Answer Directories, Inconsistent Naming

- **Primary:** `~/.hermes/profiles/tabitha/docs/Tabitha/projects/autobiography-answers/` — Days 1-8 (`2026-06-24_Day_01_Phase_01.md` format) + `day-010-answers.md`
- **Side:** `/root/docs/Tabitha/projects/autobiography-answers/` — Day 10 only (`day-10.md`)
- Different agents (Paul for early days via voice transcription, Tabitha for chat answers) captured to different locations.
- No standardized capture mechanism exists in the pipeline.

### 3. Cron Delivery: Working But Flawed

- Job `66bb141e4723` exists and delivers at 12:00 UTC to Telegram
- First two runs (July 1, 00:33-00:34) failed with "Script not found" — script was placed after job creation
- Now delivering consistently (Runs 3-5 succeeded)
- **Script auto-advances day counter regardless of answer status** — burns questions with no confirmation
- No answer-received tracking, no backlog queue, no catch-up mechanism

### 4. Joe Answered Day 10 Twice

- **Answer Set A** (from cron prompt, July 1): answered checklist questions about holidays, family wishes, best present. Saved in `day-010-answers.md`. Answers: honest but flatter.
- **Answer Set B** (from direct chat, July 2): answered his own framing about role models (Shaq, grandpa Paul), death (bullfrog/copperhead story), and sensory landscape (swamp, bark, freedom). Saved in `day-10.md`. Answers: narrative-rich, specific, the best material in the project.
- The off-script chat answers were materially better than the cron-prompt answers.

### 5. Day 9 Missing

No answer file exists for Day 9. May be recoverable from session history.

### 6. Lyra Boundary: Intent vs. Reality

The checklist questions do a reasonable job of staying in Tabitha's lane (events/context/aftermath). But Joe's best answers freely cross into psychological territory regardless — because that's where the story lives. The boundary should govern what Tabitha PROBES for, not what she ASKS about. The skill was updated to reflect this nuance.

### 7. The "Off-Script" Signal

Joe's richest answers (Day 10 chat: bullfrog, grandpa, freedom-smell) came from spontaneous conversation, not scheduled prompt delivery. The relationship is the real extraction mechanism; the cron job is scaffolding.

## Recommendations Made

1. Consolidate to ONE state file, ONE answer directory, ONE naming convention
2. Don't advance day counter until answers are saved
3. Add answer stub creation to delivery pipeline
4. Kill `/root/docs/` copies
5. Consider pull model (Joe requests when ready) over push (daily cron)
6. Add cron failure monitoring
7. Recover or re-ask Day 9
8. Build a project dashboard
