## 1. Cascade Role: Auditor AND Generator

**The cascade is BOTH.** It has a scorer built in and CAN fill slots from NEED
strings. Previous docs said "auditor, not designer" — that was an AI over-read
of Joe's frustration with early cascade output.

**Joe:** "Cascade is 100% an automation tool. We don't like using it that way
because it still needs a human touch until we work out the kinks. AI read that
as don't use it — that's my and your call."

**When to generate:** parallel faction work, volume passes, exploring V-band
possibilities before Paul designs. Output needs taste filter before lock.

**When to audit (default):** feed completed cards through for math errors,
structural drift, tone imbalance, keyword overcount.

**Rule:** Joe or Paul can say "run cascade fill on this band." Answer is yes.

**Joe:** "Mana curve is determined directly from the archetypes and function.

## 3. Gate Authority

**Any gate in the pipeline can be overridden by Joe or Paul at the end design
process.** Synergy web rules, rarity caps, keyword limits, HARD gates — all

"Rules are for the plebs."

## 4. Hero Pass Placement

**Hero pass is at the END of faction design, not during band craft.** Capo
cards are the player character — their abilities round out and reinforce ALL
design elements in the set, or tip scales to counterbalance a heavy side.

**"The cards are the bricks, the heroes are the mortar."**

**No rules other than to make cool shit.** Any constraint from earlier phases
dies here if the card is cool enough. Rapid pace — Joe scans, Paul presents,
no defending cards Joe moved past.
