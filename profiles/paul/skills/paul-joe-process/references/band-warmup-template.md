## Key Sections Explained

**What Ignites / Does NOT Do:** The "ignites" table lists mechanics that first appear or gain teeth at this band. NOT a complete mechanic list — only what's new or scaling. The "does NOT do" list prevents scope creep — mechanics that belong at higher bands, crews at target, etc. Joe will correct both.

**Draft Slot Table:** Pre-drafted slots with concept + stats + rationale. NOT final — Joe reviews and adjusts. Presents the full band at a glance. Serves as the working document during beat-by-beat design.

**Open Questions -> Resolved:** Open questions are pre-session framing that Joe answers before beat-by-beat. Move answered questions to Resolved with the answer. This tracks what was decided in-session without requiring someone to re-read the chat.

## Pitfalls

- **Don't autofill crew assignments from slot functions.** The "Crew" column should be blank or suggested, not locked, until Joe confirms. Joe may move mechanics between crews.
- **Don't treat the draft as complete.** The slot table is a STARTING POINT. Joe will adjust rarities, move mechanics between bands, and kill slots that don't earn their place.
- **Update as decisions are made.** Rare spread locked -> update the table. Bullet Time becomes a body -> move it from spells to minions. The warmup should always reflect current state, not the initial draft.
