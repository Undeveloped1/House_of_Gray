## The Refining Fire of Adversity

**Scripture:** "Not only that, but we rejoice in our sufferings, knowing that suffering produces endurance, and endurance produces character, and character produces hope." — Romans 5:3-4 (ESV)

**Stoic:** "No man is more unhappy than he who never faces adversity. For he is not permitted to prove himself." — Seneca, *On Providence*

*Both traditions refuse to see suffering as meaningless — Paul traces a chain from pain to hope, while Seneca argues that an untested life is its own kind of tragedy.*

**Reflect:**
- Think of the hardest season of your life. What did it produce in you that comfort never could have? And would you trade that formation for an easier path if you could?
- Where does this logic break? When does "suffering produces character" become a cheap thing to say to someone whose suffering has only produced wreckage — and no discernible hope?
