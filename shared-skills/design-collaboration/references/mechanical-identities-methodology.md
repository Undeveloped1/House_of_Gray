# Mechanical Identities — Clean-Slate Derivation

**Pattern developed 2026-05-26 during Bruiser PlanB session.**

## When to use

After crew domain texture is complete (lived-in detail: day-to-day, scams, hierarchy, street-level feel), derive mechanical identities BEFORE defining archetypes or functions. The crew texture IS the mechanical input.

## Process

1. Start with a clean slate — no inherited keywords from previous passes.
2. For each crew, answer: "What does this crew DO?" based on their domain texture.
3. Translate into three columns:

| Column | Question | Source |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **Mechanical Identity** | One sentence. What's the game action? | Derived from the scams and day-to-day texture |
| **Game Actions** | Specific mechanical levers. No keyword names — actions. | The holding fee → "delay, tax." The numbers racket → "enter/die effects." |
| **Feel** | One word. Then a tagline. What does it feel like to play cards from this crew? | Patient. Inevitable. Explosive. Expendable. |

4. Once all crews have identities, determine the faction archetype — what playstyle emerges from the combination?

## Example: Bruiser PlanB (2026-05-26)

| Crew | Mechanical Identity | Game Actions | Feel |
|------|---------------------|--------------|------|
| **Dock/Yard** | Controls the flow. Slows them down. Makes things wait. | Delay attacks. Force opponent to pay extra to act. Protect friendly minions while "in transit." | Patient. Inevitable. "You'll get there when I say." |
| **Hall** | Leverages position. Draws, selects, makes deals. | Draw extra. Look at opponent's hand. Symmetrical effects tilted in your favor. Protect key pieces. | Prepared. One step ahead. "I knew you were going to do that." |
| **Street** | Breaks things. Hits hard. Hits again. Grows from combat. | Deal damage. Attack multiple times. Grow stats from killing or surviving. Punish opponent for fighting back. | Explosive. Sudden. "You okay? Good. Now you're not." |
| **The Spread** | Sells access. Cheap bodies. Do something useful and die. | Cheap minions with enter/die effects. Look at opponent's hand. Force discard. Generate value on death. | Expendable. Useful. "Everybody's got a price." |

**Resulting archetype:** Fortress — combat midrange with a control spine. Dock/Yard = control shell. Hall = midrange engine. Street = midrange finisher. The Spread = tempo/attrition pieces. Survive early, dominate late.

## Key principle

The lore wrote the mechanics. A dock worker's job (holding fee) → delay/tax mechanic. A bartender's role (ears open) → scry/reveal mechanic. Ludonarrative is the input, not the polish.

## Anti-patterns

- Starting with archetype and back-filling crews to fit it
- Inheriting keywords from previous design passes without re-examination
- Naming keywords (Grit, Vendetta) before defining what the crew actually does
