# G — Art Direction Methodology

**Lore Panel Zero G:** Artist commissioning reference. Built from locked A5 (voice/tone) and A6 (visual identity).

## Medium First — The Rendering Law

**Before any artist references, palette choices, or scene grammar, lock the rendering medium.** The Five Crests art pipeline uses hand-drawn marker-line comic art with digital flat color (Clovis/Alan/Matt/Clive profiles). This is non-negotiable. Do NOT reference painters, photographers, or cinematographers as rendering references — they are for composition, lighting, and mood only.

The Clovis rendering law:
- **Line work:** Heavy, constant-weight black marker outlines. No taper, no brush variation.
- **Color:** Flat digital fills. No gradients. No blending. No texture. 3-4 dominant colors per image.
- **Shadow:** One pass. Flat. Hard edge. Single darker tone. No midtones. No bounced light.
- **Faces:** Asymmetrical, personality-driven. Expression through shape placement, not rendered detail.
- **Backgrounds:** Flat color fields or simple patterns. Same marker treatment as figure.
- **Supernatural/Villium:** Flat colored shapes with hard edges. No glow. No gradients.

**Every section below assumes this medium.** The references that follow are about WHAT to depict and HOW to frame — not HOW to render.

**Primary artist profiles (active pipeline):** Clovis (text-first, primary lane), Alan (Matt linework + Clovis color), Matt (hand-drawn baseline), Clive (richer color variant).

### 6. Compliance Checklist

Ten rules every card art must follow. These are faction-specific:

- [ ] Reads as [time period] — no anachronisms
- [ ] Conveys [faction tone] — no emotional drift (except sanctioned exceptions)
- [ ] Shows the tier — dress, environment, posture, palette must match
- [ ] Environment tells function — where they stand IS card information
- [ ] Faction visual identifier present where appropriate
- [ ] Tier-specific dress rules followed (no suits on beatniks, no gold on street)
- [ ] Movement language matches tier training
- [ ] Violence level appropriate to faction tone
- [ ] No cross-faction visual bleed (Bruiser poses on Trigger cards, etc.)
- [ ] Sanctioned exceptions documented (e.g., Court gets to be loud; Bunny gets to be emotional)

## Pitfalls

- **Mixing rendering references with composition references — THE MOST COMMON G FAILURE MODE.** Listing Hopper, Crewdson, Rockwell, and film noir cinematographers in the same "Artistic Lineage" section without distinguishing between rendering technique (how the card is drawn — marker-line + flat color) and composition/mood (how the shot is framed — lighting, tension, stillness). The reader sees Hopper and thinks oil paintings; the actual pipeline runs comic art. Always separate into two sections: Medium & Rendering (comic artists matching the Clovis style) and Composition & Mood (Hopper, noir, Leiter repurposed purely for framing/lighting direction). This was the 2026-06-04 Trigger G correction.
- **Don't just list artist names** — explain why each reference fits. "Edward Hopper" is noise. "Hopper understood that the most interesting thing in the frame is what's NOT happening — the waiting, the tension before the act" is signal.
- **Don't forget the faction visual identifier.** Every faction needs one. It's how players recognize faction cards at thumbnail size.
- **Don't make the identifier too loud.** Villium lavender is subtle — visible if you're looking, not a neon sign. The identifier should reward attention, not demand it.
- **Don't skip lighting.** Lighting IS tier information. A Management card lit like a Court card is wrong before you notice anything else.
- **Don't dress operators in suits by default.** Ask: "who do these people need to look like to do their job?" Not: "what looks cool?"
