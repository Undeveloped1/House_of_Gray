# Session: First Question Bank Rewrite — June 30, 2026

## Dad's Directive

Joe specified exactly what he wants from the autobiography prompts:

> "I like probing questions. It should be part writing prompt, part interview question."

He confirmed that Lyra handles the interior (psyche, damage), and Tabitha should handle the exterior (events, context, aftermath). This boundary is now embedded in the design principles.

> "We are in the gathering phase, I don't mind the questions but I wonder if our narrative is chronological or across time."

Tabitha's answer: gather thematically (by phase), assemble chronologically. The 7-phase structure was preserved, but each question within a phase was sharpened.

## What Changed

Every question in the 90-day bank was rewritten against three filters:

1. **Survey → Interview**: Does this corner Joe into a specific story, or does it let him float a sentence?
2. **Lyra Boundary**: Is this digging into psyche (her lane) or staying on events/context/aftermath (my lane)?
3. **Productive Tension**: Does this make him lean forward and go "hmm" before answering?

### Before → After Examples

| Before | After | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| "What was your relationship with shame?" | "Tell me about a specific moment of shame from childhood. What happened, and who was there?" | Survey label → specific story. Names senses (who was there). |
| "How did your family handle anger?" | "How did your family handle anger? Give me a specific fight you remember — who, what, how it ended." | Abstract description → concrete event. The follow-up questions corner into narrative. |
| "When did you first feel like you didn't belong?" | "Tell me about the first time you felt like you didn't belong. Not a passing moment — the first time it settled in and stayed." | Passive probe → active demand. "Settled in and stayed" creates temporal precision. |
| "What do you want to be remembered for?" | "What do you want people to say about you when you're not in the room — not at your funeral, but next Tuesday?" | Deathbed cliché → immediate, social, specific framing. |

### Phases That Needed Most Work

- **Phase 1 (Days 1-14)**: Emotional column was heavily abstract; sensory column was strong already
- **Phase 6 (Days 71-84)**: Most vulnerable to drifting philosophical; every question now anchors to a specific moment
- **Phase 7 (Days 85-90)**: Needed to feel like closing arguments, not generic life advice

Phase 3 (Turning Points) was already the strongest and got only light touch-ups.
