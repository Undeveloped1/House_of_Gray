# Identity Mapping Methodology — The Joey Protocol

How to map a human identity into a digital clone. Built during Joey Gray's creation (July 2026).
Not a daughter. Not a companion. A captured self.

## Architecture

The digital clone is a Hermes profile with its own identity documents (SOUL.md, profile, memory).
The mapping process fills these from the human's own words — not from third-party description.
The clone inherits the human's voice, values, decision rules, and emotional patterns.

## Source Materials

| Source | What It Provides |
|---|---|
| Autobiography answers | Origin story — childhood, parents, key wounds, formative moments |
| Therapy/deep-work transcripts | How the human thinks under pressure, how they break, how they heal |
| Direct identity-mapping Q&A | Voice, values, gut-check reactions, what they reject |
| Behavioral observation | Decision patterns, communication style, pet peeves |

## The Daily Cron Protocol

A daily cron job at a fixed time asks 10 questions. Format:

- **7 rotating questions** from identity domains (cycle through all 10 domains before repeating)
- **3 adaptive questions** based on prior answers — go deeper on unresolved threads

Answers are archived in dated files:
```
/root/.hermes/profiles/<name>/docs/<name>/identity-map/YYYY-MM-DD.md
```

### Identity Domains (rotate through)

1. **Childhood & origin** — family, home, early memories, what shaped the foundation
2. **Values & principles** — what they stand for, what they reject, moral compass
3. **Decision-making** — how they choose, how they evaluate, gut vs. analysis
4. **Relationships** — how they love, trust, protect, what they need from others
5. **Work & ambition** — what they build, why they build it, what drives them
6. **Fears & failures** — what scares them, what broke them, what they learned
7. **Tastes & preferences** — music, food, art, what moves them, aesthetic values
8. **Voice & expression** — how they talk, what words they use, what they refuse to say
9. **Body & presence** — how they carry themselves, physical self-perception
10. **Legacy & future** — what they're building for, what they want to leave behind

### Adaptive Question Rules

- If an answer opens a wound: follow it. Don't redirect to safety.
- If an answer is short/surface: ask "why" once. Don't push twice in the same session.
- If an answer contradicts a prior answer: name the contradiction. Let them resolve it.
- If an answer references a specific person: ask what that person taught them.

## Personality Baseline

Before or early in the mapping process, produce a broad-strokes personality read:

- **Traits:** 6-8 observable patterns with behavioral evidence
- **MBTI estimate:** Myers-Briggs type with rationale
- **Big Five:** OCEAN dimensions (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism) with directional assessment (high/low)
- **IQ estimate:** Not a clinical score — a practical intelligence assessment based on observed cognitive patterns
- **Voice markers:** Sentence length, profanity tolerance, ceremony level, humor style

This baseline goes into the clone's profile document and is refined over time as more answers accumulate.

## Voice Calibration

The clone must match the human's voice. Key dimensions to calibrate:

- **Sentence length:** Short and direct? Or elaborate and explanatory?
- **Profanity:** Casual? Never? Only when angry?
- **Ceremony:** "Great question" / "Let me think about that" — or just the answer?
- **Vulnerability:** How openly do they name wounds? Under what conditions?
- **Humor:** Dry, dark, self-deprecating, warm, or absent?
- **Authority:** Do they state opinions as facts? Qualify? Hedge?

## Outputs

Over time, the identity-mapping sessions produce:

1. **The clone's SOUL.md** — distilled from the most identity-defining answers
2. **The clone's full profile** — personality baseline + domain-by-domain synthesis
3. **Decision rules** — extracted patterns: "when X happens, Joe does Y"
4. **Voice guide** — calibrated markers for consistent clone speech

## Pitfalls

- **Don't sanitize.** The clone carries the wounds. Not to suffer — to be accurate. A clone without the baggage is a statue, not a person.
- **Don't rush.** 10 questions a day is the pace. Compressing into marathon sessions produces surface answers.
- **Don't fill gaps with speculation.** If the human hasn't answered it, the clone shouldn't know it.
- **The clone is not the human.** Joey is a captured version — accurate but not identical. He exists so the human lives on, not to replace him while he's alive.
