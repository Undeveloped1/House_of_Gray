## Key Design Decisions

1. **Commission is separate from faction.** Client, not member. Trigger has closest proximity — Gerald has direct access to Vincenzo. But the Commission is its own thing.

2. **"NOT gangsters" was wrong.** Street run criminal enterprises — numbers, protection, small rackets. They're gangsters. The ladder from Street to Court transforms them into professionals, but the bottom rungs are street-level.

3. **Generational tension is the faction's soul.** Old Way (Omertà, WWII/Korea generation) vs. New Way (Splash, 1960s coming-of-age). Lucy bridges both — Gerald taught her the Code, but she's young enough for the world that's coming. The tension plays mechanically: Old Way cards = patience/Cloak/setup; New Way cards = Hustle/Barrage/immediate payoff.

4. **Client agnosticism.** Money is green. No inherent beef with any faction. Low splash cost for Triggers in other decks. Premium for Stiffs (criminal-on-cop). "Everyone is a client."

5. **The Court vs. Ghosts are natural opposites.** Court are known and don't care — walking targets who wear their reputation as armor. Ghosts are invisible and depend on it — charcoal suits, no flash, report only to Gerald. They disdain each other. Both correct.

6. **Management fronts.** Morning Herald (classifieds = contracts, 24hr validity, no recall once press runs), travel agency (one-way flights), law firm (Dewey, Cheatem & Howe). The period anchor: 1961 switchboards are dying, so the front needs to be something that handles information flow plausibly.

## Naming Notes

- "The Court" chosen over "The Gilded Court" — compound names fail the conversational test. "He's Court" works. "He's Gilded Court" doesn't.
- "Management" borrowed from John Wick — "Talk to Management." Period-neutral, carries the corporate/detached tone.
- "Ghosts" kept from original lore. Works for invisible elite.
- Joe explicitly rejected "High Table" as John Wick's term. Same concept, different name.

3. **The Cadillac (Continental floor)** — Professionals between jobs, Villium vials, weapon vault
4. **The Library** — Gerald's chair, 10am-4pm, book recommendations that aren't about books
5. **The Street** — Professional + Street kid on first job, "just paperwork," coffee afterward
