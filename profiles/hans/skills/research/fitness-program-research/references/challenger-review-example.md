# Challenger Review — Example Output (July 2026)

Full example of a devil's advocate review of Joe's concurrent training system (fullbody lifting + BJJ).

## What the challenger reviewed

- `/workouts/fullbody-ramp.md` — Jeremy Ethier-based 3x/week full body ramp program
- `/workouts/bjj-journal.md` — BJJ training journal (Tue/Thu, purple belt curriculum)

## Key findings (summary)

### Lifting system
- Ramp protocol broken by design — Phase 1 was one session, failed immediately (ran out of time, skipped 2 of 4 exercises)
- Log ghosted after Session 3 (July 2025 — a full year gap)
- Exercise selection is generic hypertrophy — missing neck, rotational core, grip, unilateral leg work, rear delts, hip mobility, direct arm isolation
- No RPE tracking, no deloads, no periodization
- Progressive overload rules are vague (no weight increment specified, no stall protocol)

### BJJ system
- One entry in the journal — a year after lifting log started
- "No breaks for 1h10m" is a red flag, not a flex — signals either too-low intensity or training through accumulated fatigue
- Curriculum is a checklist with no study protocol, timeline, or completion criteria
- Missing: round count/duration, positional focus, subs attempted/landed/escaped, taps, partners, pre-session fatigue, post-session RPE, injury log

### Systemic blind spots
- **No integration:** Lifting plan doesn't know BJJ exists. BJJ journal doesn't know about lifting. M/W/F lift + T/Th BJJ = 5 consecutive training days with zero recovery management
- **Friday is highest injury risk day** — training on 4 days of accumulated fatigue
- **No sleep tracking, no nutrition tracking, no bodyweight tracking** (all three flagged — bodyweight and nutrition were added moments later in the session)
- **No prehab** beyond "band pull-aparts (if band available)"

### Bottom line
> "If you've actually been training BJJ Tues/Thurs and lifting M/W/F but only logged 3 lift sessions and 1 BJJ session, the logging system is the weakest link — not the programming. Fix the logging habit first."

## Prioritized recommendations (from the review)

### Immediate
1. Create combined weekly dashboard (lifts + BJJ + RPE + sleep + weight)
2. Add RPE to every lift log
3. Add "Friday lift modifier" rule based on Thursday BJJ intensity
4. Fill in or archive blank log columns

### Short-term (2 weeks)
5. Add 3–4 BJJ-specific exercises (face pulls, Pallof press, single-leg RDL, farmer's carries)
6. Structure BJJ curriculum — pick ONE combination, focus for 2 weeks
7. Program deload every 4th week
8. Fix progressive overload to explicit double progression

### Medium-term (1 month)
9. Add grip-specific work
10. Start neck training (2x/week)
11. Weekly review ritual (Sunday, 5 min)

### Long-term (3+ months)
12. Periodize the year (hypertrophy block → strength block → maintenance block)
13. Consider dropping to 2x/week lifting if recovery suffers
