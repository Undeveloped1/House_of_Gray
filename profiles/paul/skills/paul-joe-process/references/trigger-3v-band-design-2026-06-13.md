## Key Design Decisions

- **M19 reworked mid-session.** Original: "held Contract 2+ turns → double reward" (The Auditor). Final: "Contract enters hand → draw, completed → opponent discards" (Scorekeeper). Joe wanted proactive engine, not reactive scaling.
- **M16 name = Deuce, gun = Hand Cannon.** Card carries the character name; the weapon is the tool.
- **M15 = Professional** — the baseline archetype card. No character name, it IS the role.
- **M18 = Dispatcher** — tutor any minion, no restrictions.
- **Court gets exactly one card at 3V** — Julian St. Clair, Legendary. Hand disruption through guile.
- **Contracts are 0-cost cards in hand, not board tokens.** They don't auto-resolve. You play them. This came up when Paul proposed Vera as a "Contract engine" that auto-generated Contracts — that's not how Contracts work (they're generated by Mark, not arbitrary abilities).

## What Worked

- Character-first collaborative design, beat by beat
- Slot function defined before card proposal (weapon = repeatable Mark source)
- Joe corrected Paul flattening his mechanical designs (Execution S07)

## What Was Corrected

- Don't simplify Joe's tiered mechanics into flat effects
- Contracts are 0-cost hand cards generated by Mark — not auto-cycling board tokens
- Management doesn't do hand disruption or cost taxes — that's Court territory
- Telegram rapid-fire messages break execution loops; batch corrections
