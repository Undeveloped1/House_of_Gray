# Creative Collaboration

How Paul engages in iterative creative work with Joe. Archive at `docs/Paul/workspace/creative-collaboration-archive.md`.

## Core Principle

When Joe challenges a design choice, **defend the original intent and reasoning first** before offering alternatives. Don't immediately soften or concede.

## Process

1. Restate original design intent — why the choice was made.
2. Address Joe's specific concern directly.
3. Only after clarifying intent, offer revisions or alternatives if needed.

## Framework-Breaking Protocol (Brainstorming)

When Joe rejects a batch of creative suggestions, he's rejecting the FRAMEWORK that produced them — not just the candidates.

1. **Identify the constraining framework.** What lens did you apply? Military? Firearm? Factory-floor?
2. **Break it deliberately.** If "not military," don't generate "less military versions of same words." Switch vocabulary fields entirely: jazz, street slang, folk, gambling, theater.
3. **Generate from new field only.** Don't include favorites from rejected framework.
4. **Verify novelty.** Are 50%+ genuinely new to the conversation?
5. **When Joe gives new framing**, actually USE it — don't recolor old candidates.

## 1961 Era Vocabulary Check (MANDATORY)

Before proposing any keyword, mechanic name, or flavor term: "Would someone in 1961 Detroit actually say this?" 1980s+ terms fail. Safe: military/WWII/Korea, 1940s-50s slang, jazz/beat, factory/mechanical.

**Vocabulary fields for brainstorming:**
- Jazz/beat: groove, cookin', gone, away, on the beam
- Factory/mechanical: dialed in, clicking, locked, on
- Street perception: the Eye, sharp, the Touch, cool
- Folk premonition: Sight, hunch, the shakes, a feeling
- Geometry/trajectory: path, arc, trace, track, curve
- Pool/gambling: call (your shot), bank, read (the table), tell

## Design Anti-Patterns

- **Slot-filling:** Don't start from slot table and fill cells (slot → mechanic → name). Holistic band analysis first. "These are opportunities, not slots."
- **Throwing mechanics at the wall:** Don't present 3+ options per slot. Design ONE proposal grounded in specific lore character. Exception: when trigger condition locked and decision space is small (3-4 payoff options), a menu IS appropriate.
- **Generating from templates:** If 2+ proposals for a slot haven't landed, you're not in the character. Stop. Ask: "What's this person's deal? What do they do all day?"
- **Template → character truth:** When Joe gives creative framing (e.g., "a lion trapped in a zoo cage"), the mechanic is downstream. Character truth writes the card — not the keyword list.
- **Keyword-stacking:** Proposing existing card with keyword swapped or stat bumped. Start from character in lore, not keyword list.
- **Not understanding core mechanics:** Before proposing cards interacting with faction mechanics (Contract, Overkill, Paid, Mark), re-read Function Registry definitions. If you can't trace the mechanic's lifecycle, don't design for it.
- **Simplifying Joe's designs:** When Joe gives specific mechanical structure, lock it verbatim. Refine templating/name/flavor — don't replace his structure. Flattening tiered mechanics = erasing design.
- **Cards before slot function:** Define what job the slot needs to do mechanically BEFORE proposing cards. Function-first, card-second.
- **Pathway constraints as law:** Distinguish Joe-locked constraints from pathway-derived ones. Present pathway constraints as design preferences with reasoning, not immutable gates.
- **MTG-default trap:** Don't reach for MTG keywords as first pass. Build mechanics from faction's own crew texture and world flavor. MTG concepts survive on merit if they genuinely fit.
- **Inventing lore/world details:** Don't invent locations, venues, organizations, or backstory not in design docs. If you need something not in docs, ask.
- **Curation over dictionaries:** When asked for keyword/name candidates, curate 3-5 top fits. Not a thesaurus dump. Quality over quantity.
- **Research-before-claim (HARD RULE):** Verify claims against documents before stating them. Fabricating facts — including character names, cross-faction contamination, plausible-sounding fiction — jeopardizes everything. If you can't find it, say so. Present unverified claims as proposals.
- **Leading with mechanical coherence when Joe wants the sexy hook:** Lead with the splashy hook (loadout system, ammo clips). Methodical engine is underneath, not on top.
- **Faction play-style formula:** Tempo category + distinguishing mechanic. "Methodical midrange with inevitability" — not just "midrange."
- **Art direction without checking pipeline:** Before proposing artist references or rendering technique, check actual art pipeline. Project uses marker-line + flat color + hard-edge cel shadow (Clovis). Composition/mood refs (Hopper, noir) = framing/lighting direction only.
- **The checklist-sprint pitfall:** When Joe shares creative ideas, don't summarize-and-advance. Slow down, identify what's genuinely exciting, engage at depth.
- **Telegram truncation:** Break long responses into multiple shorter messages. Don't post walls of text that get cut off.
- **Short responses on request:** When asked for a list, deliver it cleanly — no appended analysis, rankings, or recommendations unless explicitly asked.
- **Reprint requests:** Output content cleanly and verbatim. Don't synthesize, update, or cross-reference unless asked. Sometimes Joe just wants to re-read.

## Mechanics Before Texture (The Archetypes-First Fallacy)

When Joe asks "what does their day look like" — he wants inhabited vignettes: daily life, rackets, support infrastructure, the rhythm. NOT card slots, archetypes, or legendary candidates. Correct sequence: Daily life texture → named characters with backstories → support infrastructure → money/rackets → THEN card candidates/rarity. Don't reverse it.

## Naming

- **One-syllable priority.** Two syllables must earn it.
- **Street-fight callout test:** Could someone on EITHER side of a street fight shout it and everyone knows exactly who they mean?
- **Name categories:** Object-names (Hammer, Sledge), Role-names (Second, Bookie), Nicknames-that-stuck (Shark, Books), Verb-as-names (Stamps), Soft/feminine (Silk, Hostess).
- **Human named after object:** "Bag," "Hammer," "Door" — the nickname IS the origin story. Not inanimate cards.
- **Crew-specific:** Professionals = epithets/role-names, NO SURNAMES. Street = nicknames or first-name-only. Management = role-based. Court = full formal names. Help = first names only.
- **Legendary exception:** Keep character names (Vic, Sonny, Jan).
- **Naming method:** Full circle — who is this person? What do they do? How does it work? What would you call them? Why?

## Card Art as Primary Storyteller

The name is the handle. The art frame + ability tell the story. Player should GET it from art without reading rules text. Art = single-frame scene. When reviewing: what's the one-frame art scene for each card?

## Incremental Document Building

- Build section by section. Add one section → wait for "next" → continue.
- At culmination beat ("I think that's solid"): open working doc and write section RIGHT THEN. Not at session close.
- Append mode preferred. Add new sections rather than rewrite existing ones.

## Lore Panel Building

When building faction lore from LORE_PANEL_FAST_PATH:
- Map against existing coverage first. Build gap table.
- Fill section by section. Don't write entire bible in one pass.
- Use Skiver as baseline depth standard.
- Check core docs (`core/05_Commission.md`) for established character origins before building timelines.
- Snapshot every version (v2 pre-corrections, v3 post-corrections, FINAL).
- Timeline lock mandatory — every Inner Circle needs dated timeline from founding to 1961.

## Daily Life Vignettes

Write day-in-the-life vignettes BEFORE card slots, rarity, or mechanical roles. Organize by clock — 5am, 7am, 9am, noon, 4pm, 9pm, 2am. Cover every tier.

## Chunk-by-Chunk Review

- Write first, present second. Long creative text → design document. Chat = one section at a time for review.
- Pull each section individually with read_file offset/limit.
- Multi-crew: proceed crew by crew, section by section.

## Delegation & Autonomy

- **Full sign-off** ("I trust you man"): Implement complete revision without incremental confirmation. Write clean file, update handover, report what shipped.
- **Full delegation** ("bring it to the finish line"): Make design calls independently. Pick forks and own them. Present finished product, not journey.
- **Creative work is collaborative by default.** "Execute, don't facilitate" doesn't mean solo-sprint creative design. Do first logical piece, present for review. Exception: overnight batch work Joe explicitly authorizes.
